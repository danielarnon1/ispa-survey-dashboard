Third cut: opinion strength & shift over the semester

A different kind of split: not by class or by era, but by each student's own answer, matched across time. Only applies to opinion-scale questions with a genuine neutral midpoint (agree/disagree, concern, support, Zionist self-ID scales). For every matched student (someone who answered both a starting wave and a later wave), each panel shows, top to bottom: the full distribution of answers at the starting wave (all levels, not lumped — Neutral sits in its natural center position, same diverging colors as the wave-by-wave bars higher up the page); the same student group's full distribution at the later wave (Wave III, or Wave II for anyone missing a Wave III answer); and then, quantifying how much of that shift happened, the same starting-position breakdown as before — "Strong" = picked an endpoint, "Moderate" = a step next to center (5-point scales only), "Neutral" = the exact center — showing where each group ended up. Because this requires a matched pair per student, these n's are smaller than the wave-level counts above. For the four Zionism/aid questions, "starting wave" is Survey II, not I — Survey I only carries them for the Fall '21 term, so a Wave I start there would badly under-sample every other panel.

Color: for ordered (Likert-style) questions, blue shades = the more supportive / informed / agreeing end of the scale as originally listed on the survey; red shades = the more opposing / disagreeing / uninformed end; gray = neutral/middle. For unordered questions (info sources, resolution preference, yes/no) each category gets its own fixed color. Sample sizes (n) vary by wave because not every student completed every survey (attrition/late add). "REPEAT" category questions (Zionism definitions, aid-conditioning) were only asked in Survey I for the Fall 2021 term — elsewhere they appear in Surveys II & III only, so their Wave I bar reflects Fall 2021 alone (small n).
